Egyptian Food Bank (EFB)

Egyptian Food Bank (EFB)

  • Food
  • 0 Comments

The Egyptian Food Bank (EFB) is a prominent non-profit organization dedicated to alleviating hunger and food insecurity across Egypt. Founded in 2006, the EFB operates on the principle of "collecting surplus food from individuals, companies, and farms, and redistributing it to those in need." Through its extensive network of volunteers, donors, and partnerships with local businesses, the EFB reaches out to marginalized communities, providing them with essential food supplies and meals. Beyond immediate food assistance, the organization also emphasizes sustainable solutions, such as education and vocational training programs, to empower individuals and families to break the cycle of poverty. With a mission to combat hunger and promote social justice, the Egyptian Food Bank plays a crucial role in addressing food insecurity and improving the well-being of vulnerable populations throughout Egypt.

Founders :

The Egyptian Food Bank (EFB) was founded by Dr. Moez El Shohdi, an Egyptian economist and philanthropist, in 2006. Dr. El Shohdi envisioned an organization that could address the issue of food insecurity in Egypt by collecting surplus food from various sources and redistributing it to those in need. With his vision and leadership, the EFB has grown into one of the leading non-profit organizations dedicated to fighting hunger and poverty in Egypt.
