Prosthetic leg

  • Medical

A prosthetic leg is a custom-made device designed to replace a missing limb, providing mobility and support for individuals who have undergone amputation due to injury, disease, or congenital conditions. These artificial limbs are meticulously crafted to mimic the form and function of a natural leg, incorporating advanced materials and engineering to optimize comfort, stability, and movement. Prosthetic legs can vary in design, from basic models for everyday use to high-tech versions equipped with microprocessors and sensors that enhance balance and adapt to different terrains.
